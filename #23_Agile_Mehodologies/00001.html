AGILE Methodologies

Agile methodologies refer to a set of principles and practices used in software development and project management. The Agile approach emphasizes flexibility, collaboration, and iterative development to deliver high-quality software solutions. It aims to address the limitations of traditional sequential or "waterfall" methods by promoting adaptive planning, continuous feedback, and incremental delivery.

Key Characteristics of Agile Methodologies:

  1. Iterative Development: Projects are divided into small iterations or time-boxed iterations, typically called "sprints." Each sprint focuses on developing a functional increment of the software.
  2. Collaboration: Agile methodologies emphasize teamwork, communication, and collaboration among team members, stakeholders, and customers.
  3. Customer Involvement: Agile methodologies encourage regular customer involvement throughout the development process to ensure the software meets their needs and expectations.
  4. Continuous Improvement: Agile teams continuously reflect on their processes and strive for continuous improvement through regular retrospectives and feedback.
  5. Adaptive Planning: Agile methodologies promote adaptive planning, where requirements and plans are continuously refined and adjusted based on feedback and changing project needs.
